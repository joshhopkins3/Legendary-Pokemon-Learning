Average Stats for Legendary Pokemon vs Average Stats for Non-Legendary Pokemon

Using Average Stats to Determine Legendary Status:

In the games, legendary pokemon tend to be stronger, faster, and harder to catch than normal pokemon. We used the pokemon's average stats (attack, defense, capture rate, HP, speed, and starting experience) as a way to help train the model to determine the legendary status of a pokemon. From these plots, we see that legendary pokemon on average have 47% higher attack, 36% higher defense, are 2.4x harder to catch, have 41% more HP, are 51% faster, and have more than 2x the starting experience than normal, non-legendary pokemon. Because these stats are significantly different between non-legendary and legendary pokemon, looking at these stats was an indicator of the legendary status.

Legendary Pokemon per Generation

A Quick Look At Legendary Pokemon by Generation:

The number of pokemon per generation varies, so it wasn't used in determining the legendary status of a pokemon. It is interesting though, to see how the number grows for just about every generation. Part of this is the introduction of evolving legendaries (for example, Cosmog in generation 7 evolves into Cosmoem, who then split evolves into Solgaleo and Lunala depending on which version of the generation 7 game you're playing (Sun or Moon)). Another aspect is the release of 'new' games that are essentially patches of the old games that introduce new pokemon mid-generation (like Ultra Sun and Ultra Moon).
